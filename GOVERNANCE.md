# Governance

VoxLattice uses lightweight maintainer-led governance.

Maintainers review contributions, manage releases, handle security reports,
and protect the project's scope, compatibility, safety, and licensing. Routine
decisions are made through issue and pull-request discussion, with technical
evidence preferred over voting.

Changes to the wire protocol, model identity, security boundaries, licensing,
or architecture require a documented proposal and explicit maintainer
approval. Backward compatibility is the default for published Python APIs and
protobuf fields.

Regular contributors may be invited to become maintainers based on sustained,
constructive participation, sound judgment, and demonstrated care for users.
Maintainer status may be relinquished voluntarily or removed for prolonged
inactivity, serious policy violations, or unresolved conflicts of interest.

Maintainers must disclose relevant conflicts and recuse themselves when they
cannot review impartially. Project conduct is governed by
[CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md), and security decisions follow
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
